Haltmann

Who put capitalism in my Kirby?

Max Profitt Haltmann, or simply President Haltmann, is a certified businessman, the ambitious and cold-hearted CEO of the Haltmann Works Company, and the main antagonist of Kirby: Planet Robobot.

Likes: his company, numerals, inventions, analyzing celestial bodies and climates, eating high-class chicken

Dislikes: opposition, other people, Kirby

Like certain real-life CEOs, Haltmann is willing to do whatever he can to make his company profit (it's in his name!). In his case, it means invading and mechanizing other planets for their resources. The last part actually sounds pretty familiar but I won't say anything else about it.

He believes he's intellectually superior to the natives of other planets and feels they are savages with no manners, which explains why he has no problems invading their homes. Yes, he's a colonizer.

Haltmann is very proud of his mech, the Executive Suit, with its gold body adorned with lilac and red gems. He is also very proud of his hovering golden chair, which he uses when he's not piloting his mech. Fit for a CEO, don't you think?

Before Kirby: Planet Robobot (Backstory)

Haltmann and his daughter, Susie, used to travel throughout the cosmos together. At one point discovered Star Dream, a wish-granting supercomputer, and its blueprints. He was able to partially reactivate Star Dream with the help of the blueprints and by researching the universe's most advanced civilizations.

Haltmann conducted tests on Star Dream regularly, until there was an accident, resulting in Susie being warped to another dimension. Believing Susie had died, Haltmann decided to spend all his time and energy into fully reactivating Star Dream. He hoped that he could revive his daughter and see her again. However, he lost his memories of her the more he relied on Star Dream.

It turned out that Susie was not dead, but when she came back, Haltmann did not recognize her. Haltmann ended up hiring her as secretary and administrative assistant of the Haltmann Works Company, as deep down he felt she was familiar.

With Susie now part of the Haltmann Works Company team, Haltmann was ready to proceed with his business plan of eternal prosperity: the Mechanization Occupation Project.

During Kirby: Planet Robobot

The Haltmann Works Company's latest mechanization target is Planet Pop Star, thank to its large amount of resources and 'uncivilized' natives. Haltmann, however, doesn't appear in person until close to the end of the game, when he introduces himself to Kirby and reveals his business plan and intents to destroy him.

Haltmann doesn't succeed (lol) and is knocked unconscious. Once he wakes up, he attempts to summon Star Dream, who absorbs his soul and possesses him. Through Haltmann' body, Star Dream states that all life forms are getting in the way of Haltmann's plans of economic prosperity.

Kirby eventually manages to destroy Star Dream' original body, which results in the end of both Star Dream and Haltmann. All of the planets that the Haltmann Works Company had mechanized were reverted to their normal states.

Non-Canon Information

Star Dream Soul OS, fought in The True Arena, begins to erase Haltmann's soul as it's no longer needed to function properly. While his soul is being erased, Haltmann wonders why he reactivated a machine as terrible as Star Dream, and ultimately remembers Susie and his wish to see her again. He regrets believing that Star Dream would have ever been able to grant that wish. Star Dream Soul OS successfully deletes all remaining traces of Haltmann, including both his memories and soul.
